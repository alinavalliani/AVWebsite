Traditonal Food Cuisines!


Pakistani food is rich, packed full of spice, generous with ghee, and unbelievably tasty.
It is an irresistible collection of dishes.
From the spiciness of the biryani to the sweetness of Kheer, every meal in Pakistan represents the nation’s love for flavorful food.

History behind Pakistani Food!

Throughout the ages, pakistani cuisine has got deeply influenced by various places.
The earliest influence connects to the Aryans and Indus Valley Civilization, where people grew different spices, such as cardamom, turmeric, saffron.
However, the arrival of Islam to the region heavily influenced Pakistani cuisine.


Pakistani Food Culture!

As a Muslim-dominant nation, the cuisine follows the religious dietary guidelines (Halal).
Thus, you will not find forbidden (Haraam) ingredients, such as pork.
Interesting fact: In Pakistan, there is a common practice of eating meals around a Dastarkhan (cloth).
At restaurants, people sit cross-legged on Takhts (raised platforms) to savor their meals.

Regional Pakistani Cuisines!

Similar to other regions, pakistan also have regional cuisines due to the presence of 4 provinces and 2 autonomous territory. Let’s evaluate each regional cuisines!


Sindh


The Sindhi cuisine comes from the natives of the province Sindh. Usually, they consume spicier chicken dishes. A famous recipe from the Sindhi cuisine is the flavorful Sindhi Biryani.

Punjab


As an agricultural hub, Punjabi food is quite diverse. However, there is a likeness for meat-oriented dishes, such as Butter Chicken. Another famous delicacy is the Murgh Cholay, in which the chickpeas and chicken get simmered with butter.

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa


Unlike the rest of the Pakistani cuisine, the KPK cuisine uses minimal spice and gives preference to the lamb meat. Some famous dishes include Chapli Kebab and Kabuli Pulao.




Balochistan

Balochi cuisine has a set of exotic dishes. People use traditional cooking methods to prepare meals, such as Dampukht and Sajji. Balochistan famous dishes include lamb-skewed Sajji and Dum biryani. One of the famous bread in Balochistan is Kaak, a rock-hard prepared bread which is often served with Sajji.

Azad Jammu and Kashmir

Meat along with rice, is the most popular food item in Kashmir. One of the famous food of Kashmir is Dum Olav or Dum Aaloo. Dum Aaloo is cooked with yoghurt, ginger powder, fennel and other hot spices to give it a unique flavour and aroma. You can have it with chapatis or naan to get the most out of this all time favourite and popular Kashmiri cuisine dish.

Gilgit-Baltistan

Gilgit Baltistan have their own traditional food which includes Harissa, Chapshuro, Buttering Daudo, Go-lee and Tumoro tea.
